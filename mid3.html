Main Learning Targets

Know what needs protection, what weaknesses exist, and what actions reduce risk.

Slide 2
What is Risk Management?

Risk management is the process of finding what could go wrong, understanding the impact, and choosing ways to protect important assets.

1

Identify

Know the assets you need to protect.

2

Assess

Check weaknesses or vulnerabilities.

3

Prioritize

Decide which risks matter most.

4

Protect

Apply controls such as updates, backups, and passwords.

Slide 3
Risk Identification: Listing Assets

An asset is anything valuable that should be protected from loss, theft, damage, or misuse.

💻

Hardware

Laptops, desktop computers, tablets, phones, routers, printers, USB drives, servers, cameras.

🗂️

Data

Grades, student records, assignments, photos, login details, research files, financial records.

🧩

Software

Learning apps, office tools, antivirus, browsers, school systems, cloud platforms, messaging apps.

Slide 4
Why Asset Listing Matters

Without an asset list...

  • Important devices may be forgotten.
  • Sensitive data may be left unprotected.
  • Outdated software may remain installed.
  • Security planning becomes incomplete.

With an asset list...

  • You know what is owned and used.
  • You can assign protection to each asset.
  • You can find critical assets faster.
  • You can reduce loss, downtime, and data leaks.
Slide 5
Vulnerability Assessment

A vulnerability is a weakness that can be exploited by accidents, malware, hackers, or careless use.

Examples of Vulnerabilities

  • Weak or shared passwords
  • Outdated software and missing updates
  • No backup of important files
  • Unsecured Wi-Fi or public network use
  • Unknown USB devices
  • Too many users with admin access

Simple Question to Ask

“What weakness could put this asset at risk?”

Example: If a laptop contains school files and has no password, the vulnerability is unauthorized access.

Slide 7
Activity: Asset Inventory

Task

List all digital assets used by the school or student. Group them into Hardware, Data, and Software.

Hardware

What physical devices are used?

Example: laptop, router, projector, smartphone.

Data

What important information is stored or shared?

Example: grades, assignments, student profiles.

Software

What apps, systems, or online tools are used?

Example: LMS, browser, email, office tools.
