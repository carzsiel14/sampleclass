InfoSec Policy Framework

Mastering the structural layers of defense: EISP, ISSP, and SysSP.

The Hierarchy of Information Security Policy

EISP

Strategic Level: High-level philosophy and governance signed by the CEO.

ISSP

Tactical Level: Detailed instructions for specific areas like BYOD or Remote Work.

SysSP

Technical Level: Configurations and ACLs applied to specific hardware.

EISP Strategic

The "Constitution" of the organization’s security.

  • Establishes organizational philosophy.
  • Assigns roles and responsibilities.
  • Ensures legal and regulatory compliance.

Information Assurance and Security 1

ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (AUP)

1. PURPOSE: To provide a secure environment for students to achieve academic excellence while protecting lab resources.

2. AUTHORIZED USERS: Enrollment-based access for SMCBI students with valid IT credentials.

3. PROHIBITED ACTS:
• Using VPNs to bypass campus filters.
• Installation of unauthorized software or tools used for "hacking."
• Accessing or storing PII of other users.

4. PENALTIES & SANCTIONS:
Failure to comply will result in:
• 1st Offense: Immediate suspension of lab account for 1 week.
• 2nd Offense: Permanent ban from lab and referral to the Dean.
• Illegal Activities: Referral to local law enforcement.

5. PRIVACY: Users have no expectation of privacy. All network traffic is monitored for security purposes.
